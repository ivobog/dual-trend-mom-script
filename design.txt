New Trend Engine v2.0
1. Multi-timeframe trend confirmation

Add optional higher-timeframe trend filter.

For example:

Chart timeframe	Higher timeframe confirmation
Daily chart	Weekly trend
4H chart	Daily trend
1H chart	4H / Daily trend

For your 1-week swing trades, I would mostly use:

daily chart signal
weekly trend confirmation

The higher timeframe should check:

weekly close > weekly SMA 30 or SMA 40
weekly SMA slope rising
weekly price not collapsing below trend support

This prevents buying a nice daily bounce inside a weak weekly downtrend.

Important implementation standard: when using request.security() for higher-timeframe data, we must avoid repaint/future leaks. TradingView warns that lookahead behavior can leak future data if used incorrectly, so v2.0 should use confirmed higher-timeframe values or safe lookahead settings.

2. Add EMA/SMA trend stack

Instead of only SMA 20/50/200, use a configurable stack:

Moving average	Role
10 EMA	Short-term momentum
20 EMA/SMA	Pullback support
50 SMA	Swing trend
150 SMA	Intermediate structure
200 SMA	Long-term trend

Trend is stronger when:

close > 20 MA
20 MA > 50 MA
50 MA > 150 MA
150 MA > 200 MA

This gives a cleaner “trend ladder.”

3. Use normalized slope, not simple slope

Current slope checks whether SMA slope is above zero. That is useful, but too crude.

Better:

slope strength = MA change over N bars / ATR

Why? Because +2% slope is meaningful for a slow stock, but maybe meaningless for a wild small cap.

New trend score should measure:

Metric	Meaning
SMA 50 slope normalized by ATR	Real trend steepness
SMA 200 slope normalized by ATR	Long-term trend improvement
Price distance from SMA 50	Healthy or too extended
Price above rising 20/50/200	Trend alignment
Higher highs / higher lows	Structure confirmation

+DI > -DI	Confirms bullish directional pressure

Good bullish trend:

+DI > -DI

This helps separate actual trend from sideways noise.

Phase 3: Upgrade momentum detection to 8/10

The biggest missing piece is relative strength.

A stock is not truly strong just because it rises. It must rise better than the market.

Phase 5: New scoring model

Instead of one blended score, v2.0 should show separated scores.

v2.0 scorecard
Score	Range	Meaning
Dual Score	0–10	Final signal quality
Trend Score	0–10	Trend strength
Momentum Score	0–10	Momentum/leadership
Setup Score	0–10	Entry quality
Risk Score	0–10	Danger level
Market Score	0–10	Broad market regime
Relative Strength Score	0–10	Stock vs benchmark

Final formula:

Dual Score =
  Trend Score * 0.30 +
  Momentum Score * 0.30 +
  Setup Score * 0.20 +
  Market Score * 0.10 +
  Risk Control Score * 0.10

Where:

Risk Control Score = 10 - Risk Score

For buy signals:

Signal	Rule
Prime Clean Pullback	Trend ≥ 8, Momentum ≥ 7, Setup ≥ 8, Risk ≤ 3
Clean Bull Pullback	Trend ≥ 7, Momentum ≥ 6.5, Setup ≥ 7.5, Risk ≤ 4
Fresh Breakout	Trend ≥ 7, Momentum ≥ 8, volume breakout true, Risk ≤ 4
Momentum Continuation	Trend ≥ 8, Momentum ≥ 8, but Setup < 7.5
Extended Momentum	Momentum strong, but extension or RSI risk elevated
Avoid	Risk ≥ 6 or distribution/failure present
